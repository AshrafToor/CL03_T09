How to Reduce Cancer Mortality Rates

Maintain a Healthy Weight

Keeping a healthy weight can lower the risk of 13 different types of cancer. Start by maintaining your current weight if you are overweight then aim to lose extra pounds gradually.

Exercise Regularly

Regular exercise is crucial for maintaining health. Aim for at least 30 minutes of activity each day.

Don’t Smoke or Use Smokeless Tobacco

Tobacco use is a leading cause of cancer. Quitting smoking and avoiding tobacco products can significantly improve your health.

Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet can reduce cancer risk. Focus on eating plenty of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains while limiting red and processed meats.

Limit Alcohol Consumption

Alcohol increases the risk of several cancers. The healthiest choice is to avoid alcohol altogether.

Protect Yourself from the Sun and Avoid Tanning Beds

Too much sun exposure can lead to skin cancer. Protect your skin by avoiding excessive sun and not using tanning beds.

Protect Yourself from Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs)

Some STIs like HPV and hepatitis can increase cancer risk. Practicing safe sex and getting vaccinated can help.

Get Screening Tests

Regular screenings can detect cancer early or prevent it altogether. Discuss with your healthcare provider which screenings are appropriate for your age and risk factors.
